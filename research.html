Health Impacts Associated
with Wildfire Smoke Exposure

Changes to the climate associated with anthropogenic emissions create conditions for longer and more active fire seasons (NOAA). The signficiance of the epidemiologically-derived relationships between smoke exposure and health effects is well documented; however, there is a great degree of uncertainty surrounding the magnitude of these health effects and in tracing the tenable relationships between individual fires and health in specific locations. I am interested in characterizing the impact of specific fires on air quality-related health. In one of my first-author papers we quantified the air quality health impacts in Brazil associated with Amazon fires.
